Responsible operations

We comply with strict national and international laws and standards - and go beyond them

- UN Compliance

Phosboucraa’s operations are in full compliance with international law and the United Nations framework.

- World Bank Compliance

OCP complies with the World Bank's Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA) standards and guidelines for all projects. We have also adopted International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). Social management is consistent with Performance Standard 2 of the World Bank International Finance Corporation, based on International Labour Organization Conventions.

- Exceeding International Standards

Phosboucraa meets and exceeds the highest standards and requirements under Moroccan law and the applicable international framework. Independent international ratings agencies have assigned high rankings to OCP Group, of which Phosboucraa is an intergral part:

VigeoEiris: 1st in emerging markets

RobecoSam: Well above the mean in all categories, 66/100 for environment

World Benchmarking Alliance: 4th/350 in the global food and agriculture

Sustainalytics: OCP is among top performers in the agricultural chemicals sector

- Moroccan Compliance

All of Phosboucraa's mining operations, products and commercial avtivities meet and exceed the highest Moroccan legal standards and industrial & commercial best practices. Across all of its activities in Morocco, OCP applies for the necessary permits and undertakes environmental impact assessments under strict local laws.

What's required under the UN Framework

The UN has established, through successive resolutions by the UN General Assembly, a clear legal framework for the people living in the Sahara and for the region itself to be allowed to economically develop while it is moving towards a final political status.

The UN framework establishes 3 criteria applicable to corporate actors:

1. The operations should promote economic advancement and provide direct and indirect benefits to the inhabitants of the territory and to the territory itself; and

2. Working conditions should be non-discriminatory; and

3. The operations sould be conducted rationally and sustainably to ensure long-term access to resources.

In 2002, the Legal Counsel of the United Nations examined this framework specifically in relation to the Sahara. He concluded that natural resource activities conducted under Moroccan law, just as are done by Phosboucraa, are legal under international law under certain conditions, namely that they meet the criteria of the UN framework. The Legal Counsel did not find that there was a requirement to conduct consultations in conducting mining and other related activities in the territory. This conclusion was reaffirmed by the UN Offuce of Legal Affairs in 2012 and 2013.

Criteria Number 3: The operations should be conducted rationally and sustainably to ensure long-term access to resources.

Over 99%
of Phosboucraa's energy consumption comes from the wind farm of Foum El Oued (in the Sahara), saving more than 80,000 tons of CO2 emissions per year. A new drying process since 2020 is saving 30% of the previous energy needs

Current desalination capacity of 1,5 cubic millimeter / year. Once the new fertilizer plant comes on stream, the total desalination capacity will reach 8 cubic millimeter / year with large excess water destined to be redistributed to the local community and the region.

Power cogeneration with excess energy destined to increase Phosboucraa's ability to desalinate waster. New facilities will include measures to minimize emissions of dust, SO2 and nitrous oxide, and to treat wastewater for industrial use and irrigation.

Preservation of phosphate resources to increase the lifespan of Phosboucraa’s reserves from 43 years to 75 years enabled by its investment in new equipment
